// proof systems explained

ZK-SNARKs (Zcash, Tornado)

STRENGTH: Mathematical Guarantee

Zero-knowledge proofs mathematically prove a statement is true without revealing ANY information about the underlying data. This is information-theoretic privacy — even with infinite compute power, the prover reveals nothing.

WEAKNESS: Trusted Setup & Speed

SNARKs require a "trusted setup" ceremony. If compromised, fake proofs can be created. Proof generation is computationally expensive (seconds to minutes). Not quantum-resistant.

Phantom Paradox (Merkle + Mixing)

STRENGTH: Speed & Cost

No ZK circuit computation. Just hashing and batching. Settlement in milliseconds. Cost approaches zero at scale. Quantum-resistant (Keccak-256). No trusted setup needed.

WEAKNESS: Statistical, Not Cryptographic

Privacy comes from MIXING, not mathematical proof. With enough surveillance (tracking all inputs/outputs over time), patterns could theoretically be detected. Anonymity depends on batch size and ghost traffic volume.

// honest assessment

// what we're NOT claiming

We do NOT claim to provide the same privacy guarantees as zk-SNARKs.

ZK proofs are mathematically stronger. They prove "I have a valid transaction" without revealing ANYTHING about the transaction. This is true zero-knowledge.

Our system provides statistical anonymity through mixing. Your transaction hides in a crowd of real + fake transactions. The more traffic, the better the privacy.

// what we ARE claiming

1. Practical Privacy: For most use cases (payments, trades), hiding in a crowd of 1000+ transactions is sufficient. Chain analysis becomes probabilistic guessing.

2. Speed: ZK proof generation takes seconds to minutes. Ours takes milliseconds. For gaming and payments, this matters.

3. Cost: ZK proofs are computationally expensive. Ours costs almost nothing. At 1M transactions, ZK would cost ~$100K. Ours costs ~$0.25.

4. Quantum Resistance: SNARKs break under quantum computing. Hash-based commitments don't.
